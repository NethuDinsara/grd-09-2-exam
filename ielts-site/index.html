Reading Section Strategies

40 Questions | 60 Minutes | Test of Scanning & Comprehension

Golden Rule for In-Order Questions

Questions generally follow passage order. Always compare the Question to the Passage, NOT the passage to the question. Heavily watch for synonym language and sentence joiners (e.g., semicolons `;` or compound clauses).

Listening Section Strategies

40 Questions | 30–40 Minutes | 4 Audio Sections

Active Prediction

During preparation time, read ahead. Predict word types (noun, verb, adjective, date, distance) for every blank before the recording starts.

Map Completion

Move your pencil along the map trace line. Focus on direction terms (opposite, adjacent, turn left) and starting reference landmarks (e.g., entrance, reception).

Distractors & Singulars

Watch for correction loops ("3 PM... oh wait, 4 PM"). Ensure 100% accuracy on singular/plural nouns (-s) and exact word-count limits.

Writing Section Framework

2 Tasks | 60 Minutes total

Task 1 (20 Mins | 150+ Words)

Weight: 1/3

Purpose: Respond to a scenario (Complaint, Invitation, Advice, Application, Request) while matching the appropriate tone.

Tone Matrix:
  • Formal: Unregistered recipient (Bank, Manager). Use "Dear Sir/Madam ... Yours faithfully". Avoid contractions.
  • Semi-Formal: Known figure (Landlord, Teacher). Use "Dear Mr. Smith ... Yours sincerely".
  • Informal: Close friends/family. Use "Dear Alex ... Best wishes". Natural contractions allowed.

Rule: Systematically expand each of the 3 bullet points provided in the prompt.

Supported Visual Types: Bar charts, Pie charts, Line graphs, Maps, Process diagrams, Tables, Multiple graphs.

Structure Blueprint:
  1. Introduction: Paraphrase the prompt graphics.
  2. Overview Paragraph: Highlight major trends, high/low points, or key transformations without detailed data.
  3. 2 Body Paragraphs: Group key data logically, providing specific stats and accurate comparisons.

Speaking Section Framework

10–20 Minutes | 3 Parts | Evaluates Fluency, Lexical Resource, Grammar, & Pronunciation

Part 1: Intro & Interview

4-5 minutes. Give immediate, direct answers followed by 1-2 supporting details. Avoid one-word answers.

Part 2: Individual Long Turn

3-4 minutes (1 min prep + 2 min speak). Use prep time to outline a narrative arc. Speak continuously until stopped.

Part 3: 2-Way Discussion

4-5 minutes. Abstract, broad societal-level analysis. Use high-level discourse markers and balance perspectives.
